Indian weddings normally are a conglomeration of various little ceremonies which could take place at the same time or one after the other. Mehendi

Date : 16.02.2017 and Time : 1900 onwards
Place : D-Block, South City 2, Sector 50, Gurugram, Haryana 122018, India

Mehendi or the tattoo painting ceremony, as known in the Western world is a ceremony taking place one day before the actual wedding. In this ceremony, the bride and all the ladies get beautiful Mehendi/Henna designs on their hands. The bride, since of course she is the bride can also get her feet painted. The men can of course get tattoos if they wish :)
Along with the Mehendi ceremony, a dinner party is organised where guests can mingle with each other, talk and dance.

Wedding Ceremony

Date : 17.02.2017 and Time : 1000 onwards
Place : Our Home(H-1/5 South City 2, Sector 50, Gurugram, Haryana, 122018, India)

Traditionally, the Indian wedding ceremony takes quite a long time. We, on the other hand, have cut it short to a 2 hour ceremony. Don't worry, you are not required to sit and your attendance is not mandatory but nonetheless, we would really appreciate if you could join and view the ceremony firsthand.

The ceremony starts with the“Arrival of the Groom’’ with his family and close friends to the“Bride’s home”. This is traditionally supported with some traditional Indian music where the Groom and his family and friends are expected to dance on arriving at the Bride’s home.

Once reaching at the bride’s home, the“Groom Party’’ is greeted by the bride’s parents. During this time, the bride is busy trying to get ready. The families and friends are then welcomed to the place where the ceremony has to take place. This place is called a Mandap.

The bride and the groom sit along with their parents in the Mandap and perform various little rites and ceremonies which form the Indian wedding.

Significance of the Fire

A small fire is lit in the middle. Agni or Fire Deity holds a significant place in Hindu culture. As a fire deity, Agni serves as a passage of communication between the humans and gods. In the wedding ceremony, Agni serves as the primary witness to the union of the bride and groom. It is believed that Agni consumes ritualistic offerings, and transfers them to the respected gods through smoke.

Various little rituals make the up 2 hour ceremony. However, I will try to explain the three most important rites :

Kanyadaan

Kanya means girl in Hindi, daan means to give. The words Kanyadaan means giving away of the daughter by her father. This is very similar to the western ritual when the father walks the bride down the aisle to the groom.

Panigrahana

Pani means hand, and grahana means acceptance. The bride and the groom voluntarily take each others hand near the fire to signify their union.

Saptapadi

This is the famous Indian ritual which involves going around the fire 7 times and exchanging vows. Sapta means 7, padi means steps. Why 7 ? I am not quite sure, but the bride and groom exchange 7 vows with each other, which range from supporting each other financially, physically mentally and spiritually. Marriage is considered the ultimate friendship in the Hindi tradition and after the seven vows, the marriage is complete and the bride and groom are husband and wife.
