Where to go before or after

India ! My motherland and home to one of the most beautiful and chaotic places in the world!

Since you people have decided to attend our wedding, it would just make sense for you to extend the stay to a great fat Indian vacation. There are of course two possibilities, you can either decide to come some time before the wedding or stay after the wedding. In any case, we would highly recommend you to stay at least for 2 weeks if not more. We have picked some places which we believe make up for a definite visit when you come to India for the first time.

Exploring the North

The north of India is abode to one of the highest mountains, the Himalayas in the world. If you are a mountain lover and would like to take a peek into the Himalayas, Nainital in a nice place to start.

Nainital is a hill station in the Indian state of Uttarakhand, and sits at an altitude of 2084m. This trip is however not recommended for people who tend who have a bit of a motion sickness.

Distance from Delhi : Around 300km Transportation from Delhi : Train, Bus, Personal Car

We have mostly travelled with the train to Nainital and the itinerary could look like this:

Day 1

  • Leave for Kathgodam from Old Delhi Railway Station with Delhi-Kathgodam Shatabdi Express. The train leaves at 6am in the morning and arrives in Kathgodam at 12 ( Provided its not late )
  • Take a taxi from Kathgodam to Nainital , around 30km ride
  • You can relax and look around the city

Day 2

  • There is a high altitude zoo in Nainital which you can visit.
  • You can even boat in the Naini Lake.
  • There is also a small (only 2,615 m) Naina Peak you can climb. I would definitely recommend this, because on a clear day, you get to see the whole Uttarakhand Himalaya (even till Nepal).

Day 3

  • You can potentially hire a taxi and visit the nearby regions of Almora (you get to see Nanda Devi which is the tallest peak residing fully in India), Bhimtal, Sattal (7 lakes region).

On our last mountain trip we went with this company and we were really happy with how they took care of us and arranged the whole thing: bikatadventures.com. If you’re looking for a hiking trip this is the best way to enjoy it.

Moving to the West

The Thar Desert lies to the west of Delhi, in the semi-arid state of Rajasthan. I must say, February would be a nice time to visit Rajasthan, since its not hot. The tourist place closest to Delhi, lying in Rajasthan is the capital of Rajasthan called Jaipur.

Jaipur is also easily reachable with a 4 hour train journey from New Delhi Railway Station. You could potentially spend 3-4 days in and around Jaipur.

Main highlights are the palace of Jaipur, the Hawa Mahal and the Amber Fort. Another very awesome place is the Chowkidani, where you need to spend atleast one evening. The people of the state have remodelled a whole modern village in Chowkidani and you get to experience the culture of Rajasthan first hand. A great warm place where you get great Rajasthani food (A LOT OF IT), dance, music. You could also spend a night in one of the village homes there which of course will have all the basic amenities.

Other places in Rajasthan, include Udaipur, another beautiful city close to Jaipur. But of course you need a taxi/train to reach to Udaipur. We, personally haven't been yet to Udaipur but I have heard its also a nice place to visit.

Jodhpur, Bikaner, Jaisalmer are cities further deep in the desserts. You could also do a dessert tour. I haven't yet done it, but I am sure its worthing doing a dessert safari there.

And of course, the South with its Beaches

Goa

For people who just love beaches, India has a lot to offer, and of one of the most popular and common places for foreign tourists to visit is of course, Goa ! February is a nice month to visit South of India because the monsoons have retreated and you have plenty of sunshine and humidity ! The water in the Arabian Sea will be quite warm, around 30°C, so yes, quite warm.

I, personally, have never been there, but its not a hidden fact that its quite a party place.

How to reach : Train, Flight 

Unless you want to take a train ride on the Konkan coast for more than a day( which I have done quite a lot of times, and it is quite beautiful) , I would recommend you to fly there. There are daily flights going from Delhi to Goa. You can book them on any search engine like momondo.com or makemytrip.com. Tickets would cost around 100-120 euros if I am not mistaken.

You can make a trip of 4 days in Goa as well. Get lazy on the beach, go visit the fort in Goa, do some water sports….There are quite a lot of things you can do there. Since it was a Portuguese colony, I am sure you will see quite a lot of european architecture there.

Kerela

Kerela is another beautiful state in India. They call it the“God’s own country’’ in India. I have visited Kerela, several years ago and I can confirm that its quite a pleasant place with very beautiful natural surroundings.

You can fly to Kochi in Kerela from Delhi, and from there start your travel of Kerela. There is a nice town, called Alapuzzha, mostly known as Venice of the East, simply because it has great back-water lakes and canals connecting the whole town.

If you are interested in going to a natural sanctuary, you can even travel to Periyar National Park and do quite some jungle safari and a hike there.

Again, reserve atleast 4-5 days to do all this. You will have to take trains from Kochi to Alapuzzha and also to Periyar.
